About the Prophet

Prophet Muhammad (PBUH): A Life of Mercy and Wisdom

Born in Mecca in 570 CE, Prophet Muhammad (Peace Be Upon Him) is the final messenger of Islam. His teachings emphasize compassion, justice, and devotion to God. His life continues to inspire millions around the world, promoting harmony, charity, and the pursuit of knowledge.

  • Emphasized kindness and mercy towards all creatures.
  • Advocated for justice and fairness in society.
  • Praised the value of education and wisdom.

History of Eid Milad-un-Nabbi

Eid Milad-un-Nabbi commemorates the birth of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). Celebrated on the 12th day of Rabi’ al-Awwal, it is observed with prayers, sermons, and community gatherings worldwide. Traditions include reciting poetry, sharing food, distributing charity, and decorating mosques with lights and banners.

The celebration originated in the early Islamic period and has evolved to include regional customs, such as processions and musical performances, fostering unity among Muslims.
